23-Descriptions about Basotho Traditional Music and Dances

Basotho culture is rich in vibrant music, captivating dance performances and fascinating traditions. Festivals and celebrations play a crucial role in showcasing and preserving this cultural heritage.

Traditional Basotho Music tells stories of life, struggle, love and history

Traditional Dances

1.Mokhibo

Performed by women,kneeling down and moving shoulders rhythmically.

2.Mohobelo

A group of male dance with legs moving together in perfect timing to create visual effect.
Their movement and stamping is the same showcasing unity and power among them.

3.Liphotha

Performed during initiation rituals, cultural festivals and national celebrations.
It is done by men with both singing and dancing.

4.Selia-lia

A group of boys and girls join together to perform it is usually played i order for one to get a partner.
Boys have to be on their side forming a line

5.Litolobonya

Performed by women, characterized by slow, graceful movements and deep emotional meaning often in cultural festivals.
Litolobonya is often used to xepress respect, celebration, gratitude or excitement

6.Ntlamo

A traditional dance that is performed by men and young boys.
When played both men and young boys do not wear any form of shoes.

7.Mokopu

It is played by girls wearing a traditional attire called pokoma that is white in color with white t-shirts and they do not wear shoes.

8.Setepe

A Basotho dance that is performed by dance wearing skirts during festival celebrations.
Setepe is regarded as a Basotho dance that is compulsory for every Mosotho child in the rural areas.
It is also taken as a form of entertainment whereby these girls compete with different girls from other regions.

9.Mokorotlo

A men's war dance with synchorized leg movements and stomping

10.Likhapela

Dance performed by couples during courtship or weddings.

11.Kiba tsa Basotho

Men`s dance in acircle with rythmic stamping and swinging of sticks.

12.Sehong-hong

Children's traditional play dance.

Traditional instruments

1.Meropa

Used in ceremonies and dances to keep rhythm

2.Sekhankula

Used in dances, it is made with a tin, a wooden stick, a string made from animal gut.
Men are usually the ones who have play it using a stick to produce a soft sound for dancing.

3.'Mamokhorong

A wooden stick with a wooden stick and a resonator.
It is often used in Basotho traditional music and is played by men and boys to accompany singing or storytelling

4.Lesiba

Is made from a long feather quill or stiff wire attached to a string.
It is played by inhaling or exhaling sharply across the string, causing it to vibrate.
The player places the string against the lips and uses the mouth as a soundbox.
Blowing air over the feather or wire.

5.Lekolulo

An instrument that is flute-like often played by young boys while herding livestock.

6.Setinkane

A Basotho instrument that is more of a thumb piano that produces melodic sounds.

7.Thomo

A traditional instrument in a form of a string that played by women and girls.
This instrument women and girls use it during personal expression.

8.Setolo-tolo

Mostly played by men,it is played using a mouth blow .

9.Lifare

An instrument that produces melodic sounds used in celebrations.
Lifare is an instrument made from wood.

10.Sekebeku sa ts'epe

Made from steel or iron,it has a pear like shape and has a blue straight line inbetween that is also made of steel.

11.Khabulu-bulu

It is made of wood like stick that has a string on it.
It is played by young people such that they swing the instrument in a circular motion to produce a humming sound.

12.Koriana

An instrument that features keys that is played by men during cultural celebrations.
When being played , a man has to carry it on his chest in order for him to produce an entertaining song for everyone.
In most cases it is played during a girls dance that is known as setepe.

13.Lekhitla la lesapo

Lekhitla la lesapo is an animal's bone that a hollow hole is made inside in order for a player to blow inside to produce a sound

14.Lekhitla la lenaka

It is more of an animals horn

15.Mooa

A horn or trumpet made from animal horn.
Used in ceremonies and celebrations.

16.Phala-phala

Horn trumpet

17.Matlhotlhoe

An instrument that is tied on ankles filled with seeds.
